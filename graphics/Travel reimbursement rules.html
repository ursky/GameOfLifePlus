Travel Reimbursement

The preferred method for booking travel is through the Concur Travel Tool. The preferred way to pay for items while on business travel is with a Corporate Travel Card. All expense reports must be submitted in accordance with policy through the Concur Expense Tool.

Air Travel

Book the lowest logical airfare in the economy class of service, unless that fare does not include a guaranteed seat (such as "saver" fares). If you would like to fly Business Class, you can pay for the ticket out of pocket and request reimbursement for the lowest logical economy class airfare available at the time of booking.

See below for additional tips and answers to frequently asked questions around submission of Airfare expenses:

The following are examples of expenses which are reimbursable:

  • Seat assignments, overhead bin fees, and checked luggage if flying on a carrier that does not include those items in their standard economy fare
  • In-flight wireless access for business purposes
  • Frugal meal or drink purchases if meal service is not included in the fare
  • Travel visas and associated passport fees when traveling for business

When parking at airports, if available, please use on-site parking to avoid additional contact. If not available, travelers should utilize offsite parking when possible. Take advantage of our online discount coupons. For current COVID-19 cleaning and preventive measures, visit the companies' websites. Additional travel guidance can be found on the Travel Suppliers' Preventative Measures Overview on our COVID-19 Travel Wiki.

The Expense Reimbursement Team supports Body Positivity. Those seeking accommodation for travel can submit a request by email.
  
The following are examples of expenses which are not reimbursable when booking airfare or at check-in:

  • Service upgrades (such as Economy to Premium Economy)
  • In-flight entertainment
  • Airport lounge charges
  • Security clearance services such as TSA Pre-Check or Global Entry
  • Seat assignment fees for changes from a standard seat to a preferred seat

All spend should be made with frugality and business ethics in mind. 

Cancellations should be made in accordance with the instructions listed on the travel itinerary. Cancellation charges are not reimbursable without appropriate justification and approval from employee's business L8+. Approval must be attached to the expense report along with necessary receipts.

Use of personal aircraft while on Amazon business is prohibited, and will not be reimbursed.

Lodging

In compliance with Tax requirements, a hotel folio and proof of payment showing zero balance due is to be provided when submitting expense reimbursements.

When submitting expenses, only the room rate and room taxes should be claimed under lodging and all other incidentals should be itemized and listed separately (for example, room service – meals).

The following are examples of expenses may be reimbursable with appropriate justifications:

  • ​​Reasonable in-room food and beverage charges
  • Laundry charges 
  • Mobile Phone charges in accordance with policy and listed local requirements
  • Valet parking
  • Business center fees

The following are examples of expenses which are not reimbursable during hotel stays:

  • Spa charges
  • Personal entertainment such as movies
  • Room upgrades
  • Personal points spent on upgrades

Employees are encouraged to consider amenities provided by the property as part of Amazon's negotiated rates such as free breakfast, self-parking, and gym facilities. 

Cancellations should be made in accordance with the instructions listed on the travel itinerary. No-show/cancellation charges are not reimbursable without appropriate justification and approval from employee's business L8+. Approval must be attached to the expense report along with necessary receipts. 

Rental Car

All car rental reservations should be made in accordance with the Global Travel Policy. Amazon will reimburse for up to intermediate size. Provide justification for reimbursement requests for larger or upgraded vehicles as they may be sent back to the employee if business need is not provided. GPS may be reimbursable with proper business justification. 

Satellite radio, motorcycles, scooters, and bicycles are non-reimbursable expenses.​

Rail Travel

If the costs of booking your train tickets are paid directly by Amazon through our Travel Agency Partners and your credit card is not charged, expense submission for the ticket cost is not required. In some countries, a company railway card may be an option for frequent business travelers to minimize expenses to Amazon.

Meals while on Company Travel

The “Meals" expense type refers to an individual's actual meals and snacks incurred and consumed while traveling on Amazon business. As a guideline, average daily meal spend incurred by employees while traveling is about USD $50 per day, depending on the city or country of travel. In certain countries, there is a maximum amount that can be reimbursed based on local tax requirements.

Mileage and Tolls

Use the mileage calculator within the Concur Expense Tool when submitting for mileage reimbursement. Mileage reimbursement rates factor in gas, oil, and maintenance costs and therefore these costs should not be expensed.

Local mileage rates for all countries are updated annually and can be viewed here. 

Associates can expense mileage and tolls beyond their normal commute. Regular commuting between home and assigned office location cannot be expensed. 

Taxis and Shuttles

Taxis, shuttles, or other transportation services such as Uber, Lyft, or Car2Go are the preferred method of ground transportation while traveling on business where car rental services are not applicable. Limo and private car services are not reimbursable. Ensure receipts submitted for these expenses contain the origin, destination, date and amount.

FAQs and Contacts

For concerns regarding travel expenses, please open a ticket to the Expense Reimbursement Team that is appropriate for your concern by picking from those listed here. For travel related concerns check out the Travel Expense FAQs or visit the Travel Team's wiki.
